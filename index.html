ABOUT

Muse are an English rock band from Teignmouth, Devon, formed in 1994. The band consists of:

  • Matt Bellamy - lead vocals, guitar, keyboards
  • Chris Wolstenholme - bass guitar, backing vocals
  • Dominic Howard - drums

They signed to Maverick Records and released their debut album, Showbiz, showcasing Bellamy's falsetto and a melancholic alternative rock style. Their second album, Origin of Symmetry, incorporated wider instrumentation and romantic classical influences, and earned them a reputation for energetic live performances. Absolution saw further classical influence, with orchestra on tracks such as "Butterflies and Hurricanes", and became the first of five consecutive UK number-one albums.

Black Holes and Revelations incorporated electronic and pop elements, displayed in singles such as "Supermassive Black Hole". The album brought Muse wider international success. The Resistance and The 2nd Law explored themes of government oppression and civil uprising and cemented Muse as one of the world's major stadium acts. Their seventh album, Drones, was a concept album about drone warfare and returned to a harder rock sound. Their eighth album, Simulation Theory, prominently featured synthesisers and was influenced by science fiction and the simulation hypothesis.

Muse have won numerous awards, including two Grammy Awards, two Brit Awards, five MTV Europe Music Awards and eight NME Awards. In 2012 they received the Ivor Novello Award for International Achievement from the British Academy of Songwriters, Composers and Authors. As of June 2016, they have sold over 20 million albums worldwide.

TIMELINE

  • 1994 - As Rocket Baby Dolls they won a local battle of bands and changed their name to Muse afterwards.
  • 1998 - Released their first serious recording, Muse EP on Sawmills Studio's in-house Dangerous label, produced by Paul Reeve.
  • 1999 - Second EP, the Muscle Museum EP, was released. Later in that year they performed at Emerging Artist's stage at Woodstock '99
  • 1999 - Debut album Showbiz has been released, reaching number 29 on the UK Albums Chart.
  • 2001 - One of the greates rock albums of 2000s, Origin of Symmetry has been released, recieving positive reviews by critics.
  • 2002 - First live DVD Hullabaloo and double album Hullabaloo Soundtrack featuring gigs at Le Zenith in Paris, was released.
  • 2003 - Muse's third album Absolution debuted at no.1 in UK and was certified gold in the US. It featured first top-ten hit Time Is Running Out.
  • 2006 - Black Holes and Revelations released charting no.1 in UK, much of Europe and Australia.
  • 2007 - They became first band to sell-out Wembley Stadium, which was recorded on CD / DVD titled HAARP, released early 2008.
  • 2009 - Fifth album The Resistance released, first produced by the band, for which they have received Grammy Award for Best Rock Album.
  • 2012 - The 2nd Law released, including official song for London 2012 Summer Olympics Survival.
  • 2013 - Released their fourth live album Live at Rome Olympic Stadium on CD, DVD and Blue-ray formats.
  • 2015 - Band returned to simpler rock sound on new album Drones, which won another Grammy Award for Best Rock Album.
  • 2017 - Eight studio album Simulation Theory released, which incorporates influences from science fiction and 1980s pop culture.
